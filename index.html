Executive summary

TXST published 22 long-form videos and 30 Shorts in FY26 (Sept 1, 2025 – present), excluding live streams and podcast episodes. Across both formats, content reached well above 90% non-subscriber audiences, confirming YouTube's role as a top-of-funnel discovery channel rather than a subscriber retention tool. Institutional highlight videos drove the strongest video performance, while campus life and experiential content led Shorts. The pre-FY26 benchmark for Shorts — a student-purchased dorm tour — remains the highest-liked piece of short-form content in the dataset, reinforcing the value of authentic, campus-specific storytelling.

Notable patterns

Videos

  • Institutional highlights dominate. The top two videos — Each and Every One and TXST in 2025: Record Success — are broad-audience institutional pieces that likely benefited from email or organic amplification beyond the YouTube channel itself.
  • Sharp drop-off after #3. Views fall from 1,852 at #3 to 1,008 at #4 and 567 at #5, suggesting the top performers had unusual reach drivers that most FY26 videos did not.
  • Each and Every One generated 111 shares — by far the most of any video in the dataset — and skewed heavily toward the 65+ age group (34.8%). This suggests emotional resonance and possible alumni or community sharing behavior.
  • TXST Autocomplete Pt. 1 had the highest average view duration (7:35) in the top 5, indicating strong watch-through despite a lower view count — a signal of content quality that view numbers alone don't capture.
  • Pre-FY26 benchmark (Drug Resistant Fungal Infections) has 17,363 views but an average view duration of only 0:40, indicating mostly browse-off behavior. High views with low retention typically signals search/recommendation discovery without sustained interest.
  • The Battery Evolves (163 views) is a niche research topic. Low performance is expected for subject-matter-specific content and should be benchmarked separately from broad-audience institutional videos.

Shorts

  • Campus life content performs best. The top short (TXST from all angles, 10,075 views) and the pre-FY26 benchmark (Falls Hall Dorm Tour, 11,326 views) both focus on campus experience — consistent with a prospective student and family audience.
  • The pre-FY26 benchmark still outperforms all FY26 Shorts. Falls Hall Dorm Tour leads all shorts in likes (188) and views (11,326). It was purchased from a student creator with compensation based on Instagram view count — a model worth revisiting for future content acquisition.
  • 25–34 is the dominant age group across the top 3 Shorts (54.4%, 27.3%, and 50.5% respectively). This is slightly older than a traditional prospective student audience and may suggest alumni, parent, or young professional engagement.
  • Ring Ceremony at TXST (9 views) is flagged, not failed. This short was uploaded in May 2026 and has been blocked worldwide due to copyrighted music. The view count is not a meaningful performance signal.
